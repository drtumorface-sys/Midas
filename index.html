Getting Started
What MIDAS Does
MIDAS is a meditation and brainwave tool. It plays two slightly different tones — one in each ear — through your headphones. Your brain naturally hears the difference between them as a slow pulse. That pulse can guide your brainwaves toward specific states: deep sleep, relaxation, focus, or heightened awareness.

Everything in the app — the sounds, the visuals, the colours — is generated from a single word you type in. The word becomes a number, the number becomes a frequency, and the frequency shapes the entire session.

You need stereo headphones. Earbuds or over-ear — either works. Speakers will not produce the effect.
Step by Step
Running a Session
1. Type a word — Enter any word or short phrase into the Phonosemantic Catalyst field and press TRANSCODE. This converts your word into the base frequency for the session. Every word produces a unique tone and visual pattern.

2. Choose a visual pattern — Below the central canvas, pick one of four sigil styles: Phyllotaxis (plant-spiral geometry), Kamea (magic-square grid), Ogham (ancient Irish script), or Radial (geometric vectors). These are visual meditations — each one also slightly shifts the carrier tone.

3. Pick a brainwave mode — The Cortical Entrainment Protocol section has 8 frequency buttons plus a Cycle Mode. Each one targets a different mental state. See the mode guide below.

4. Pick a breathing pattern — The Vagal Induction Cycle section offers guided breathing rhythms. These are optional but recommended — controlled breathing amplifies the effect of the tones. Any breathing pattern works with any brainwave mode.

5. Choose a carrier source — Under Carrier Derivation, use either your word's frequency (Gematric Seed) or a fixed reference tone (Lithic Anchor). Lithic anchors are pre-set frequencies tied to natural and musical reference points.

6. Press INITIATE PROTOCOL — The session timer starts, the visual pattern animates, and the breathing guide begins. Toggle the acoustic field on separately with the audio button below.

7. Adjust volume — Use the Field Depth slider to set a comfortable listening level. Start quiet and increase gradually. −18 dB is a good starting point.

Breathing Guide
How the Breathing Patterns Work
The ring around the central sigil expands and contracts to guide your breath. Each pattern is shown as three numbers: inhale – hold – exhale (in seconds).

Breathing out for longer than you breathe in activates your vagus nerve — the main nerve responsible for calming your body. Slower breathing (around 6 breaths per minute) also synchronises your heart rate and blood pressure, creating a state of deep physiological balance.

You can use any breathing pattern with any brainwave mode. Experiment to find what feels right. For deep relaxation, try a long exhale (like 4-0-8 or 6-2-10). For balanced calm, 5-0-5 or 4-2-6 work well.

Science
How Binaural Beats Work
When your left ear hears one frequency and your right ear hears a slightly different frequency, your brain creates a "phantom" third tone at the difference between them. This is the binaural beat.
Left ear = f₀ − Δf/2  ·  Right ear = f₀ + Δf/2
For example: if the left ear gets 100 Hz and the right ear gets 110 Hz, your brain perceives a 10 Hz pulse (Alpha range). This phenomenon is called the frequency-following response (Galambos & Makeig, 1981) — your brain's electrical activity naturally synchronises to match that beat frequency. It's measurable on an EEG.

The base tone (carrier) is kept between 75–150 Hz — low enough that your ear doesn't try to identify it as a musical note, and well within the range where binaural perception is strongest (below 1,500 Hz).

Descent modes (Coherence, Delta, Theta) slow your brainwaves down toward sleep and deep rest. Ascent modes (Alpha, Beta, Gamma) bring you back toward alertness and sharp focus.

Audio Engineering
The Signal Chain
Every tone passes through five processing stages before reaching your ears:

Thermal Saturation — Adds warmth by gently rounding the waveform (like a vacuum tube amplifier). The formula f(x) = ((1+k)x)/(1+k|x|) at k=25 generates even harmonics that sound warm without the buzzy distortion of hard clipping. "Attenuate" mode reduces this to k=15.

Resonance Lattice — Three hidden tones at the base frequency, a perfect fifth above, and an octave above. They sit below conscious hearing but give the sound a richer, more "alive" quality through a phenomenon called stochastic resonance.

Lithic Enclosure — A synthetic reverb that simulates a stone cave (1.8-second decay, heavy high-frequency damping, 30% wet mix). Gives the sound a cavernous, subterranean feel.

Velvet Curtain — A low-pass filter at 300 Hz that removes all high-frequency content. The result is pure sub-bass and low-mid weight — you feel it as much as hear it.

Sovereign Limiter — A compressor that prevents the audio from clipping (distorting) when multiple tones overlap.
